## Main conclusions of the novelty audit

1. The study should not claim that use of citizen-science images or inference of plant traits from photographs is unprecedented.
2. A close current predecessor exists for multimodal continuous trait inference from citizen-science photographs, but the located version is a 2025 preprint.
3. The defensible novelty is the joint treatment of a repeated floral observation unit, continuous multi-module traits, trait-specific assessability, within-species distributions, species-specific climate slopes, among-species environmental sorting and uncertain phylogenetic placement.
4. The literature supports describing Carduus–Cirsium as systematically, cytogenetically and reticulately complex; it does not support calling the whole genus a demonstrated rapid adaptive radiation.

## Reproducibility note

This log records search concepts and retained papers, not a formal systematic-review protocol. The manuscript is an empirical research article rather than a systematic review. Claims labelled as novelty claims must therefore remain bounded to the literature located and should use wording such as `few studies`, `remains uncommon` or `we found no previous study combining...`, never an unqualified `first`.

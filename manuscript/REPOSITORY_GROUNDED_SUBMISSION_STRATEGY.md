# Repository-grounded submission strategy

Review date: 2026-07-17

This document records the submission position supported by the executed repository. It does not infer a journal target from reputation, impact factor or topic similarity. A named journal is not yet frozen anywhere in the repository.

## 1. Paper identity

### Central claim

Species means conceal a scale-dependent and modular capitulum phenotype: visible within-species variation, within-species environmental responsiveness and among-species environmental sorting are distinct structures.

### Paper type

The manuscript is a conceptual comparative-macroecology paper enabled by public-image phenomics. It is not primarily:

- a global trait atlas;
- a detector or computer-vision benchmark paper;
- a new species-tree paper;
- a demonstration of adaptive radiation;
- a causal test of rain protection, pollinator selection, plasticity or local adaptation.

### Biological system

*Cirsium* is used as a stringent test because the genus is morphologically diverse and historically complicated by taxonomic uncertainty, hybridization, polyploidy and incomplete direct representation in dated backbones. These features motivate distributional and uncertainty-aware analysis but are not inferred causes of the reported trait patterns.

## 2. Evidence that defines the manuscript

The submission-facing evidence base is:

- 6,626 detected capitula from 3,725 public observations;
- 216 accepted image-analysis taxa;
- nine primary continuous endpoints spanning orientation, colour and outline;
- 46,276 spatially thinned observations from 259 taxa in the strict within-species layer;
- 102 taxa in the complete two-axis lability cohort;
- 36 grouped SPDE-INLA models;
- 50 alternative grafting trees, 522 historical PGLS fits and 636 phylogenetic-signal fits.

The headline results are frozen:

1. approximately 82–99% of visible variance occurs within species;
2. species within-variation and environmental responsiveness are negatively associated across 102 complete taxa (Spearman rho = -0.333);
3. colour has the highest median responsiveness but lowest median variation;
4. shape has the highest visible variation without the highest responsiveness;
5. environmental niche separation is strongest for orientation and visible colour and weaker for most outline traits;
6. the strict <=10 km cohort has zero BH-supported primary effects;
7. the expanded pooled coefficient table has four small BH-supported effects;
8. apparent historical signal is sensitive to direct-backbone coverage and grafted placement.

## 4. Submission gates in priority order

### Gate A — detector and measurement credibility

Required outputs:

- independent detector precision, recall and error categories;
- assessable and unassessable examples for each trait module;
- tight-crop and context-crop examples;
- orientation, colour-mask and shape overlays generated by production measurement functions;
- horizontal-flip examples;
- low, middle and high values for each endpoint;
- licence-verified panel inventory.

Infrastructure for this gate is implemented by PRs #45 and #46. The remaining task is to provide and audit the source-image artifact and completed manual tables.

### Gate B — taxonomic freeze

Commit one decision table containing source name, accepted study name, external match, match type, decision, decision reason and evidence source. Approximate, synonym and multiple-match records require explicit human decisions. Taxonomic changes must not silently alter the submitted analysis.

### Gate C — spatial robustness

Add residual spatial diagnostics for headline among-species models and a broad-region or spatial-block sensitivity summary. These results define how strongly the paper can discuss environmental sorting versus geographic turnover.

### Gate D — manuscript assembly

After Gates A–C:

1. write `02_methods.md` from the actual implemented and validated pipeline;
2. write the Discussion using the frozen seven-part architecture;
3. revise Introduction and Results for cross-section consistency;
4. complete figure and table captions;
5. add data availability, code availability, contributions and acknowledgements;
6. validate every headline number against `final_claims.json`.

### Gate E — durable release

Create a permanent archive containing final tables, figures, manifests, software environments, workflow identifiers, commit SHA and SHA-256 checksums. Preserve observation identifiers and licence fields without redistributing photographs beyond their licence terms.

## 5. Journal-selection strategy

The repository does not yet specify a target journal. A journal should be selected only after the manuscript package is complete enough to compare its actual scope against journal requirements.

The target must accept the following paper identity:

- conceptual macroecology or comparative ecology;
- plant functional or reproductive traits;
- global citizen-science or biodiversity-image data;
- hierarchical separation of within- and among-species processes;
- methods that support, but do not dominate, the biological contribution;
- observational and explicitly non-causal inference;
- extensive supplementary validation and reproducibility material.

Reject a target when its expected paper identity would require any of the following:

- a demonstrated causal mechanism;
- experimental evidence of adaptation or pollinator selection;
- a resolved species phylogeny;
- a primarily computer-vision contribution;
- removal of the within-species distributional result in favour of a simple trait atlas;
- claims stronger than the frozen repository evidence.

### Decision rule

Rank candidate journals only after checking their current aims, article types, word limits, figure limits, data policies and recent comparable papers. Record those facts in a dated journal matrix. Do not choose a target from memory or from an earlier informal list.

## 6. Claim vocabulary

Use:

- visible within-species variation;
- environmental responsiveness;
- climate tracking;
- among-species environmental sorting;
- module-dependent;
- scale-dependent;
- historical-placement sensitivity;
- reticulate or polyploid evolutionary context.

Do not use as conclusions:

- local adaptation;
- phenotypic plasticity;
- pollinator causation;
- rain-protection adaptation;
- evolutionary rate;
- adaptive radiation;
- hybridization or polyploidy as demonstrated causes;
- resolved *Cirsium* species tree.

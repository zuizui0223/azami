# Title and abstract

## Preferred title

**Species means conceal scale-dependent and modular variation in thistle capitulum traits**

## Abstract

Comparative trait ecology commonly represents species by mean values, although dispersion among observations, within-species spatial environmental associations and among-species environmental sorting need not describe the same structure. We developed a public-image phenomics framework for globally distributed *Cirsium* observations that retained detected capitula, trait-specific measurement failure, spatial provenance and uncertain historical placement. A balanced image-comparison atlas contained 6,626 detected capitula from 3,725 observations and 216 accepted image-analysis taxa. An exhaustive all-photo stream yielded 406,582 detector-positive observations; 46,276 observations from 259 taxa remained after a ≤10 km coordinate restriction and taxon-by-cell spatial thinning. We quantified nine continuous endpoints spanning capitulum orientation, visible corolla colour and two-dimensional outline. Across the image-comparison atlas, approximately 82–99% of visible variance occurred within assigned species, although this quantity includes biological heterogeneity, viewpoint and measurement components. A reviewer-style audit showed that the previously used RMS of unpooled absolute species slopes was strongly confounded with slope sample size and standard error, so the associated negative correlation and median-split quadrants were withdrawn. In a revised 101-taxon complete cohort, a hierarchical variance meta-regression that incorporated every slope standard error detected no common change in latent environment–trait slope magnitude with visible variation (log-variance coefficient = -0.042, 95% profile CI -0.274 to 0.200; likelihood-ratio P = 0.732). A transparent sampling-noise-adjusted, equal-module score gave the same conclusion (Spearman's rho = -0.057, 95% species-bootstrap CI -0.265 to 0.155). Environmental sorting remained strongest for orientation and visible colour and weaker for most outline traits. In the exhaustive spatially thinned cohort, four non-circular linear endpoint–climate associations passed BH correction but were small; four hue-component rows also passed component-wise correction and require joint circular interpretation. Apparent historical signals were sensitive to grafted taxon placement. Public-image phenomics therefore reveals distributional and scale-dependent structure hidden by species means, while showing that comparisons of cross-species environmental association must model sampling precision explicitly.

## Claim boundary

The manuscript does not claim demonstrated phenotypic plasticity, local adaptation, selection, pollinator causation, evolutionary rate, rain-protection adaptation or a resolved *Cirsium* species tree.

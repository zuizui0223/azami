# Title and abstract

## Preferred title

**Species means conceal scale-dependent and modular variation in thistle capitulum traits**

## Alternative title

**Public photographs reveal decoupled variation and climate tracking in a reticulate thistle lineage**

## Abstract

Comparative trait ecology commonly represents species by mean values, although phenotypic dispersion, within-species environmental tracking and among-species environmental sorting need not describe the same biological structure. We developed a public-image phenomics framework for globally distributed *Cirsium* observations that retained individual detected capitula, trait-specific measurement failure, spatial provenance and uncertain historical placement. The image-analysis layer contained 6,626 detected capitula from 3,725 public observations and 216 accepted taxa. We quantified nine continuous endpoints spanning capitulum orientation, visible corolla colour and two-dimensional outline, and analysed a separate strict within-species climate layer containing 46,276 spatially thinned observations from 259 taxa. Across the nine primary endpoints, approximately 82–99% of visible variance occurred within species, although this quantity includes biological heterogeneity, viewpoint and measurement components. Among the 102 taxa meeting the predeclared completeness criteria, species within-variation and environmental responsiveness were negatively associated (Spearman's rho = -0.333), and all four high/low combinations were well represented. Colour showed the strongest median climate tracking but the lowest median total variation, whereas shape showed the greatest visible variation without the greatest responsiveness. Environmental niche contrasts were strongest for orientation and visible colour and weaker for most outline traits. No primary effect passed BH correction in the strict <=10 km within-species cohort, while four small effects passed correction in an expanded pooled table. Apparent historical signals were sensitive to grafted taxon placement. Distributional, scale-explicit and module-aware image phenomics therefore reveals ecological structure hidden by species means, while requiring explicit treatment of assessability, spatial scale and historical uncertainty.

# Target journal strategy

## Recommendation

### 1. Global Ecology and Biogeography — primary target

Best fit if the manuscript is framed as a general macroecological result about how species-level trait summaries conceal scale-dependent structure.

Why it fits:

- broad geographic and taxonomic scope;
- comparative and spatial analyses;
- explicit within-species versus among-species scaling;
- a general conceptual conclusion relevant beyond *Cirsium*;
- public-image phenomics as an enabling approach rather than the sole contribution.

Required framing:

- lead with the general problem of species means in comparative ecology;
- present *Cirsium* as a global test system rather than the endpoint;
- foreground scale dependence, module dependence and environmental sorting;
- avoid letting image-processing detail dominate the Abstract or title;
- make the first four figures carry the general macroecological claim.

Main editorial risk:

- the paper may be viewed as taxon-specific unless the Abstract and Discussion make the general implication explicit;
- weak strict within-species effects must be presented as a scale-dependent result, not as a failed climate-association study.

Preferred title direction:

> Species means conceal scale-dependent and modular variation in thistle capitulum traits

## 2. Journal of Ecology — co-primary / strongest plant-ecology target

Best fit if plant reproductive-trait ecology and the biological contrast among orientation, colour and shape are emphasized.

Why it fits:

- plant ecology is central;
- the paper addresses general ecological principles rather than only a taxonomic survey;
- the data connect plant reproductive traits to biotic and abiotic environments;
- the distributional treatment of intraspecific variation is relevant to plant trait ecology.

Required framing:

- retain the specific orientation, colour and shape results;
- discuss why reproductive trait modules differ in visible variation, climate tracking and environmental sorting;
- position public imagery as a way to extend plant trait ecology beyond sparse species means;
- keep mechanistic claims cautious because the study is observational.

Main editorial risk:

- the paper may be judged insufficiently mechanistic if it reads mainly as image macroecology;
- detector and measurement credibility must be especially clear.

Preferred title direction:

> Public photographs reveal decoupled variation and climate tracking in thistle capitulum traits

## 3. Ecology — strong and realistic broad-ecology target

Best fit if the paper is presented as a concise empirical test of a general ecological concept: phenotypic dispersion and environmental responsiveness are not equivalent.

Why it fits:

- broad comparative and observational ecological approaches are accepted;
- the paper develops a generalization potentially applicable to other taxa;
- conceptual novelty does not depend on a fully resolved mechanism.

Required framing:

- keep the manuscript concise;
- centre the two-axis result and biological-scale contrast;
- move detailed workflow, phylogenetic and molecular audits to the supplement;
- emphasize the general ecological phenomenon rather than journal-specific plant language.

Main editorial risk:

- a long methods-heavy manuscript could dilute the ecological result;
- too many parallel analyses could make the story appear exploratory.

## 4. Methods in Ecology and Evolution — only under a different manuscript strategy

Not the preferred destination for the current manuscript. The journal explicitly prioritizes the new method and its uptake over the biological results obtained by applying it.

A competitive MEE paper would require:

- independent detector and trait-measurement validation;
- clear benchmarking against alternative methods;
- a reusable software package or documented workflow usable outside *Cirsium*;
- uncertainty propagation and transferability tests across taxa or datasets;
- the biological results used mainly as a demonstration.

This would be a substantially different paper. The current Chapter 1 should not be distorted into a methods paper unless the validation work is expanded.

## 5. Nature Ecology & Evolution — aspirational first shot only

The scope includes macroecology, integrative comparative biology, ecophysiology and phylogenetics, but the journal selects only advances of exceptionally broad interest.

A submission could be justified only if the final paper convincingly changes how comparative trait ecology represents species, rather than reporting a rich case study in thistles.

Minimum standard for a credible attempt:

- exceptionally clear evidence that species means lead to qualitatively different ecological conclusions;
- a simple and striking main figure architecture;
- detector and measurement validation complete;
- robust spatial diagnostics complete;
- strong demonstration that the framework generalizes beyond the focal lineage, or a compelling argument that it changes trait-database practice;
- a concise manuscript accessible outside plant ecology.

Current assessment:

- within scope;
- high desk-rejection risk;
- no need for a primary-research presubmission enquiry unless scope itself is uncertain;
- worth considering as an ambitious first submission only after the credibility gates are closed.

## Journals not prioritized

### Functional Ecology

The present study is observational and does not directly identify mechanisms. Functional Ecology emphasizes significant mechanistic or process-oriented advances and is therefore a weaker fit unless experimental or physiological evidence is added.

### Journal of Plant Ecology

A reasonable safety journal with broad coverage of plant–environment relationships, macroecology, evolutionary ecology and methods. It is not the first choice because the current paper has a stronger general macroecological claim than a specialist fallback requires.

## Recommended submission ladder

### Impact-maximizing route

1. Nature Ecology & Evolution — optional ambitious first shot;
2. Global Ecology and Biogeography;
3. Journal of Ecology;
4. Ecology;
5. Journal of Plant Ecology.

### Fit-and-efficiency route

1. Global Ecology and Biogeography;
2. Journal of Ecology;
3. Ecology;
4. Journal of Plant Ecology.

## Final decision rule

Choose **Global Ecology and Biogeography** if the final Abstract begins with the general failure of species means and ends with a macroecological implication.

Choose **Journal of Ecology** if the final Abstract and Discussion devote equal weight to the biological ecology of orientation, colour and shape.

Choose **Ecology** if the manuscript is shortened around the two-axis lability result and biological-scale mismatch.

The current recommendation is to write the paper to **Global Ecology and Biogeography** specifications while preserving enough plant biological detail to transfer cleanly to **Journal of Ecology**.
